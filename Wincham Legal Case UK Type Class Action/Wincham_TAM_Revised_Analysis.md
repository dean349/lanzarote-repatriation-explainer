### 2.2 Head 2 — Corporate Banking Failure (Proceeds in Wrong Account)

Head 2 is **directly and materially property-value-dependent**. The loss is:
- **Additional tax cost** = (Dividend tax rate 39.35% − Correct CGT rate 24%) × net sale proceeds received into personal account
- **Lost FTCR** = Spanish CGT already paid, now unrecoverable because personal receipt negates corporate CGT treatment
- **Professional rescue costs** = MVL restructuring, tax specialist advice, HMRC correspondence

This head only applies to **victims who have sold or are selling**. Based on the scheme's history (operating since mid-1990s) and the ATED pressures accelerating exits, we estimate **40–60% of the 860 victim companies have already sold or are in the process of selling.**

| Calculation Component | Philip Harrison (€346,500, £295k net) | Average Victim (€495k, £421k gross) |
|---|---|---|
| Gross sale proceeds | €346,500 = **~£295,000** | €495,000 = **~£421,300** |
| Estimated net proceeds after Spanish costs (3% retention, agent fees, notary ~8% total) | ~£236,500 | **~£346,500** |
| **Additional tax — dividend vs CGT treatment**: (39.35% − 24%) × net proceeds | (15.35%) × £236,500 = **~£36,300** | (15.35%) × £346,500 = **~£53,190** |
| **Lost FTCR** — Spanish CGT paid (~19% × gain ~60% of sale price) | Spanish CGT on £236k gain portion ≈ ~£17,600 | Spanish CGT on £346k gain portion ≈ **~£24,200–£28,600** |
| **Professional rescue costs** (tax specialist, HMRC, MVL restructuring) | £5,500–£16,500 | **£5,500–£16,500** |

1. **With law firms:** The revised TAM of £68.5M base case makes this a **top-quartile commercial group litigation opportunity** in UK professional negligence. Comparable GLOs (Giambrone, Blemain Finance, Excel Independent) have total pools in the £20M–£80M range. This sits at the top of that target zone.

This deserves specific legal opinion — but if correct, it opens an FCA/FOS route in addition to the courts.

4. **For media strategy:** A story about 1,720 British pensioners with an average of £421,300 tied up in a failed tax scheme — cumulative losses potentially exceeding **£68.5 million** — is a national newspaper story. Financial Times, The Times, and consumer finance publications will run this. Media pressure on Wincham's PI insurer is a powerful accelerant toward settlement.
