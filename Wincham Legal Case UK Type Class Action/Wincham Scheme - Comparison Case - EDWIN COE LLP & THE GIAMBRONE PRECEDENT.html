The Precedent Case

Giambrone & Law — The Group Action

Calabria, Italy | 2007–2025 | High Court, Court of Appeal, PI Insurer Battles

🏛️ The Scheme

In the mid-2000s, the Italian law firm Giambrone & Law — with offices in London, Milan, and Palermo — marketed off-plan luxury holiday apartments in Calabria, southern Italy, to hundreds of British and Irish investors. The key developments were:

  • "Jewel of the Sea" — Calabria
  • "El Caribe" — Calabria

UK investors — predominantly middle-class retirees and pre-retirees — paid deposits of £30,000 to £105,000 each, expecting completion by 2009.

🔑 The Critical Structural Fact Giambrone & Law acted as the clients' own solicitors throughout — i.e., the firm trusted by the investors to protect them was the same firm that failed to warn of the catastrophic risks it was aware of. This is the precise archetype of the Wincham matter.

⚠️ The Four Failures Courts Found

  • Failure to advise on risks: Giambrone knew the Calabrian construction sector had links to the 'Ndrangheta (Calabrian Mafia) and the Provisional IRA. It failed to disclose this.
  • Failure of due diligence: Inadequate investigation of planning permissions, title, and developer financial stability.
  • Breach of trust / fiduciary duty: Released client deposits to developers without obtaining contractually-required guarantees — when the developments failed, the money was gone.
  • Continued to act without warning: Even as red flags mounted, the firm kept billing and taking fees.

🚧 The Giambrone Recovery Problem — And Why Wincham Is Different

The Giambrone case hit a wall that made recovery extremely difficult and prolonged:

  • Giambrone & Law went insolvent. This meant claimants who won in court still couldn't access the money easily.
  • AIG attempted to aggregate all claims into a single £3M policy limit — which would have paid each claimant pennies. This fight alone lasted years.
  • The result: Claimants won in 2015–2017 and are still in recovery litigation in 2025. That is 16 years.
⛔ Giambrone Recovery
  • Firm insolvent — no direct assets
  • AIG aggregation dispute lasted years
  • £3M policy limit vs. multi-million loss
  • Still litigating in 2025
  • Many claimants never fully recovered
✅ Wincham Recovery
  • Active, solvent, trading firm
  • Live PI policy — paying insurer
  • PI insurer already settled OOC twice
  • GLO = existential threat to firm
