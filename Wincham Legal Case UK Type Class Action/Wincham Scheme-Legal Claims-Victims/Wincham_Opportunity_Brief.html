02 — Legal Basis

Why Victims Have Good Cases

Four independently strong heads of claim attach to every victim in the database. Any authorised CMC picking this up has a ready-made pre-packaged litigation case.

📋

Professional Negligence

Wincham owed a duty of care when advising on tax structuring. They failed to advise on the catastrophic consequences of Brexit on their offshore wrapper scheme. Foreseeable loss, breached duty, direct causation.

Caparo Industries plc v Dickman [1990] — 3-stage duty test satisfied
🚫

Unauthorised Legal Practice

Mark Roach and Wincham International held themselves out as tax and legal advisers. They were not qualified solicitors, barristers, or registered tax practitioners — making all paid advice potentially unlawful and void.

Legal Services Act 2007 · ICAEW Bye-Laws · Solicitors Act 1974
💬

Misrepresentation

Wincham marketed the scheme as a lawful, compliant tax-saving structure. This was not true. Active misrepresentation of the scheme's legality and sustainability gives victims a statutory rescission and damages claim.

Misrepresentation Act 1967 s.2(1) — negligent misstatement
🔐

Limitation Window — Still Open

The 6-year limitation clock (Limitation Act 1980) runs from the date of knowledge — not when the scheme was set up. For many victims, HMRC penalty letters and MVL forced costs only crystallised post-2020. The window is open now.

Limitation Act 1980 s.14A — latent damage, 3 years from knowledge
03 — Commercial Strategy

Two-Phase Revenue Model

Phase 1 generates immediate cash. Phase 2 captures the full long-term upside. Both phases leverage the same irreplaceable asset: the Wincham victim database.

⚡ Phase 1 — Immediate (Months 1–6)

Licence the Data · Get Hired as the Marketing Firm

Approach one established FCA-authorised Claims Management Company (target: Manchester-based specialist). Offer them two things simultaneously: a non-exclusive Data Licence for the victim database, and a Marketing Services Agreement to run all victim outreach on their behalf. This is commercially clean, legally compliant, and generates income within weeks — not years.

  • 1Data Licence Agreement: Dean licenses the Wincham victim database to the CMC for a one-off upfront fee (£15K–£50K depending on negotiation). Dean retains full IP ownership. The licence is non-exclusive and time-limited. Lawful basis: database compiled from Companies House public records (lawfully available) — no personal data processing issue arises at the licence stage.
  • 2Marketing Services Contract: Simultaneously, Ellis's UK company enters a Marketing Services Agreement with the same CMC. Dean subcontracts all the actual marketing work under that agreement, invoiced from the US. The CMC gets outreach capability. We get 65–70% of all revenue generated from Dean's work, zero UK tax exposure.
  • 3Ellis's UK Company as Appointed Representative (AR): Ellis's UK Ltd registers as an FCA Appointed Representative of the CMC (the Principal Firm). This is a standard arrangement — the principal holds the FCA authorisation, the AR operates under their regulatory umbrella. No FCA direct authorisation required at this stage.
  • 4Revenue Share on Claims: Negotiate a revenue split on any Wincham claims that convert — the CMC takes the regulated case management fee (20–35% of recovery), Ellis's AR company takes a 20–25% cut of that CMC revenue, and Dean takes 65–70% of Ellis's cut via the marketing subcontract.

🚀 Phase 2 — Medium Term (Months 6–18)

Get FCA Authorised — Run the CMC Ourselves

The long-term value capture is to cut out the principal CMC entirely. With Ellis as FCA-authorised CMC director and Dean as the commercial and marketing engine, the full 35–40% claims management fee flows to us — not shared with a third-party firm. The FCA authorisation process for a CMC is demanding but achievable in 6–12 months with the right compliance infrastructure.

  • 1Set Up Ellis's UK CMC Ltd: Incorporate a dedicated CMC vehicle (separate from any AR entity). Ellis is sole director and holds the Senior Management Function (SMF). Written compliance policies, client money account, and Professional Indemnity Insurance are required before FCA application.
  • 2Submit FCA Direct Authorisation Application: Apply under FSMA 2000 s.19 for permission to carry out the regulated activity of managing claims. The FCA's CMCOB sourcebook governs all conduct obligations. Realistic timeline: 6–12 months from submission to approval.
  • 3Panel Solicitors Agreement: Appoint a panel of qualified litigation solicitors (DBD model — no win, no fee). The CMC manages the client acquisition, claim preparation and litigation co-ordination. The panel solicitors manage court proceedings. CMC fees come from their success fee.
  • 4GLO Application: Co-ordinate with panel solicitors to apply to the Business and Property Courts for a Group Litigation Order. The Wincham database becomes the claimant register. Once the GLO is granted, all claims are managed collectively — massive economy of scale.

04 — UK GDPR & Data Compliance

The Data Question — Why It's Simpler Than It Looks

The Wincham database is built from publicly available Companies House records and AEAT company data. This is not a secret — it's the same public record any solicitor or journalist could access. UK GDPR does not prohibit using public records commercially; it simply requires a documented lawful basis. We have three.

🛡️

Three Valid UK GDPR Lawful Bases Apply No Loophole Required

GDPR Art. 6(1)(f) — Legitimate Interests

Connecting victims of a fraudulent scheme to legal redress is a legitimate interest that overrides individual privacy rights. The ICO accepts this basis for legal claims databases. A Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA) is documented and kept on file.

GDPR Art. 6(1)(b) — Contractual Necessity

Once a victim signs a CMC retainer agreement, all data processing under that contract has a clear contractual basis. The licence agreement triggers this basis for downstream processing by the CMC.

Publicly Available Data — Reduced Obligations

UK GDPR Recital 47 & ICO guidance confirm that personal data already made public — including Companies House director records — attracts reduced obligations. No special categories, no sensitive data involved in the Wincham database.

📌
The Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) Requirement When the database is transferred from Ellis's UK Ltd to Dean's US entity for marketing work, Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) must be executed. This is a standard two-page document — not a barrier. Every UK company doing business with a US service provider completes these. The CMC's data protection solicitor will draft it as routine.
