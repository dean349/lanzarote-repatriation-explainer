PART 1 — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY OF ALLEGATION

This referral concerns a group of UK-registered companies trading under the "Wincham" brand that are alleged to have:

  1. Conducted regulated financial intermediation activity (cross-border Spanish property investment advisory services) without FCA authorisation, in breach of the General Prohibition (FSMA 2000, section 19);

  2. Operated a financial intermediation holding company (Wincham Investments Limited, SIC code 64999) without FCA authorisation, in breach of FSMA 2000, section 19;

  3. Permitted an Appointed Representative (Belgrave Wincham Limited, FRN 912897) to operate outside the scope of its Appointed Representative approval, allowing unrelated Wincham group entities to benefit from the regulatory credibility of the AR relationship without being subject to it; and

  4. Made misleading representations to clients about the regulatory status of the Wincham group, causing clients reasonably to believe they were receiving regulated financial advice from an FCA-authorised firm when they were not — a potential breach of FSMA 2000, section 25 (misleading statements as to the authorisation of regulated activities).

The affected client group is estimated at approximately 1,606 households — predominantly UK-resident British nationals who held Spanish property (in Spain or the Canary Islands) through UK limited companies administered by the Wincham group between approximately 2004 and the present. These are not expatriates; they are UK-domiciled property investors who visited their Spanish holdings as visitors within legal stay limits. The total financial harm to clients is estimated at between £50 million and £88 million (as calculated in supporting professional negligence documentation).

PART 3 — THE REGULATED ACTIVITIES CONDUCTED WITHOUT AUTHORISATION

3.1 — The General Prohibition (FSMA 2000, Section 19)

Section 19 of FSMA 2000 provides:

"No person may carry on a regulated activity in the United Kingdom, or purport to do so, unless he is an authorised person or an exempt person."

A breach of the General Prohibition is a criminal offence under section 23, carrying a maximum of 2 years imprisonment and/or an unlimited fine.

3.2 — Regulated Activities Conducted by the Wincham Group

The Wincham group provided the following services to UK clients holding Spanish property through UK limited companies:

3.3 — The SIC 64999 Issue: Wincham Investments Limited

Wincham Investments Limited (Co. 02948944, CW12 4TR) has operated under SIC code 64999 — Financial intermediation not elsewhere classified since at least the 2000s. This SIC code is within the FCA's regulatory perimeter.

A company registered under SIC 64999 that is conducting financial intermediation in or from the United Kingdom must hold FCA authorisation under FSMA 2000, unless it falls within a specific exemption (such as the exemption for group treasury companies under Article 70 of the RAO, which does not apply here).

Wincham Investments Limited: - Is not on the FCA Register (verified April 2026); - Has no applicable exemption; - Has operated as the apex holding company of a group providing financial intermediation services to retail clients (British Spanish property investors) for over 30 years; - Remains active to this day.

PART 4 — THE BELGRAVE WINCHAM APPOINTED REPRESENTATIVE: SCOPE AND MISUSE

4.1 — What the FCA Register Shows

Belgrave Wincham Limited (FRN 912897) is an Appointed Representative of Thornton Springer Ltd (the directly authorised principal). Under the FCA's Appointed Representative regime (FSMA 2000, section 39), an AR may conduct regulated activities on behalf of its principal, within the scope of the appointment agreed with the principal. The principal firm (Thornton Springer Ltd) is responsible for the AR's regulated conduct.

4.2 — The Concern: Group-Wide Use of the AR Credential

The FCA Register lists Belgrave Wincham Limited as an AR. However, Belgrave Wincham Limited is one of five entities in the Wincham corporate group. The concerns are:

  1. The Wincham group's marketing materials and client engagement created the impression that the entire "Wincham" group was regulated — not merely Belgrave Wincham Limited. Clients who searched for "Wincham" on the FCA Register may have encountered FRN 912897 and reasonably concluded that "Wincham" as a whole was FCA authorised. This is a misleading indicator of compliance.

  2. The scope of Belgrave Wincham's AR appointment is restricted to regulated activities agreed with Thornton Springer Ltd. The broader Wincham group's activities — particularly the cross-border Spanish property advisory and TCSP activities — are unlikely to fall within this scope. If any regulated activity was conducted by other Wincham entities under the colour of Belgrave Wincham's AR status, this constitutes an unauthorised extension of the AR permission.

  3. The principal firm, Thornton Springer Ltd, as the FSMA 2000 s.39 responsible principal, may itself have failed in its supervisory obligations if it permitted or failed to prevent Belgrave Wincham from operating outside its appointed scope, or if it allowed the Belgrave Wincham AR status to be used as group-level regulatory cover.

4.3 — The FCA's Supervisory Interest

The FCA has a supervisory interest in: - Whether Thornton Springer Ltd conducted adequate diligence on Belgrave Wincham Limited before appointing it as an AR; - Whether Thornton Springer Ltd has adequate ongoing oversight of Belgrave Wincham's activities; - Whether the scope of Belgrave Wincham's AR appointment extends to the cross-border property advisory activities that appear to have been conducted across the Wincham group; and - Whether any of the Wincham group's client-facing activities were misleadingly described as "FCA regulated" when they were conducted by entities other than Belgrave Wincham Limited.

PART 5 — CLIENT HARM: THE PHILIP HARRISON CASE AS AN ILLUSTRATIVE EXAMPLE

Philip Harrison, the Complainant, is a retired British citizen who has been permanently domiciled and resident in the United Kingdom throughout the entire period of his ownership of Los Romeros Limited. He has never been a resident of Spain. He and his wife held a 100% joint shareholding in Los Romeros Limited, and owned a holiday property (a villa in Lanzarote, Canary Islands) which they visited freely and lawfully, in compliance with British and Spanish law governing permitted stay durations for British nationals — both pre- and post-Brexit. Philip Harrison's status as a UK-resident, non-resident owner of Spanish property (not a Spanish resident or expatriate) is material: Wincham's fiscal representation obligations were specifically those owed to a UK-based, non-EU non-resident — a category whose regulatory treatment changed materially on 31 December 2020 following Brexit.

Philip Harrison held his Lanzarote property through Los Romeros Limited (Co. 06993349), administered by Wincham Accountants Limited.

Philip Harrison engaged Wincham's services in reliance on the reasonable belief — supported by Wincham's marketing, its use of an ICAEW-affiliated accountant, and the existence of a Wincham-branded FCA registration (Belgrave Wincham, FRN 912897) — that he was receiving professionally regulated financial and tax advice.

He was not informed that:

  • The entity through which he contracted (Wincham Accountants Limited) held no FCA authorisation;
  • The FCA-registered entity (Belgrave Wincham Limited) was an Appointed Representative only, and its regulatory cover did not extend to the activities Wincham performed for him;
  • The apex holding company of the group (Wincham Investments Limited) was conducting financial intermediation (SIC 64999) without FCA authorisation;
  • No member of the Wincham group was registered with HMRC as a Trust or Company Service Provider under the Money Laundering Regulations 2017.

PART 7 — SPECIFIC REQUESTS TO THE FCA

The complainant respectfully requests that the FCA:

  1. Open a supervisory investigation into whether Wincham Investments Limited (Co. 02948944, SIC 64999) has been conducting regulated financial intermediation activity without FCA authorisation, in breach of FSMA 2000, section 19;

  2. Investigate whether Agere International Ltd (formerly Wincham International (UK) Ltd, Co. 06441315) and/or Wincham Accountants Limited (Co. 05607266) conducted regulated financial advisory activities (including cross-border Spanish property investment advice and post-Brexit restructuring advice) without FCA authorisation, in breach of FSMA 2000, section 19;

  3. Review the Belgrave Wincham Appointed Representative relationship (FRN 912897, principal: Thornton Springer Ltd) to determine:

  4. Whether Belgrave Wincham's activities fell within the agreed scope of its AR appointment;
  5. Whether Thornton Springer Ltd exercised adequate supervisory oversight as principal; and

  6. Whether the Wincham group's marketing and client communications misleadingly gave the impression that all Wincham entities were FCA-regulated by reference to FRN 912897;

  7. Consider whether a section 384 restitution order under FSMA 2000 is appropriate in respect of clients who received unregulated financial advice from the Wincham group and suffered loss as a result;

  8. Consider whether the conduct constitutes a criminal offence under FSMA 2000, section 23, and if so refer the matter to the Crown Prosecution Service or, in respect of the cross-border element, to the National Crime Agency;

  9. Add the Wincham group entities to the FCA Warning List pending the outcome of the investigation, to protect retail consumers from further harm;

  10. Notify the complainant (or their instructed solicitors) of the outcome of the FCA's assessment of this referral, including whether an investigation is opened.
