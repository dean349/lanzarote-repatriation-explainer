BACKGROUND

A. The Licensor has assembled a proprietary database of approximately 1,564 records across 782 UK limited companies and their directors that were incorporated and administered by Wincham Accountants Ltd (dissolved) and its successor entities, including Adrem Accounting Ltd, across two registered addresses (CW12 4TR and CW12 4AA, Congleton, Cheshire), using data sourced from publicly available registers including Companies House (the "Wincham Victim Database"). A forensic cross-reference completed April 2026 confirmed zero overlap between the two address cohorts — these are entirely separate victim populations.

B. The Licensor wishes to grant the Licensee a licence to use the Wincham Victim Database for the purpose of identifying and engaging potential claimants in professional negligence proceedings against Wincham Accountants Ltd and associated entities.

C. This Agreement sets out the terms on which that licence is granted, the consideration payable, and the obligations of the Parties.

1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Licensed Data" means the Wincham Victim Database as delivered to the Licensee pursuant to clause 4, comprising company records, director details, company status information, and associated data fields, sourced from Companies House and other publicly available UK registers.

1.2 "Permitted Purpose" means using the Licensed Data solely to identify, contact, and engage potential claimants for the purpose of providing legal services in connection with professional negligence claims against Wincham Accountants Ltd, Adrem Accounting Ltd, and their successor or associated entities.

1.3 "Identified Person" means any director, shareholder, or associated individual whose details appear in the Licensed Data.

1.4 "Deferred Payments" means the payments described in clause 5.2 below, contingent on the Licensee achieving defined litigation milestones.

1.5 "Personal Data" has the meaning given in the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) as retained by section 3 of the Data Protection Act 2018.
