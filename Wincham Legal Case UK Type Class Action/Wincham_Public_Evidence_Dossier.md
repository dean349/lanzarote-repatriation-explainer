## EVIDENCE ITEM 1: The Olive Press — Primary News Article

### Publication Details
- **Publication:** The Olive Press (Spain's only English-language newspaper, cited by NYT, BBC, Sky News, The Times, The Guardian)
- **Author:** Antonio Flores, Legal Columnist, Lawbird Solicitors (practising since 1997, Marbella)
- **Date:** 17 April 2014
- **URL:** https://www.theolivepress.es/spain-news/2014/04/17/spanish-tax-office-closes-inheritance-tax-avoidance-schemes/
- **Title:** *"Spanish tax office closes inheritance tax avoidance schemes"*

### Full Article Text (Verbatim)

> *"SPANISH inheritance tax (IHT) is once again a matter of controversy. According to El Pais newspaper, the number of people who have given up their inheritance has gone up by 21% since last year. And there is only one reason cited for this increase: the inability of many potential inheritors to pay a tax bill unless they first inherit and sell the asset in question, generally a property, in a very challenging market.*
>
> *Unfortunately for many, Spanish IHT and ways to avoid it has been exploited relentlessly by unscrupulous operators to sell new schemes promising that this tax would no longer be a problem.*
>
> *The Spanish Equity Release comes to mind just now but also, more recently, **the bogus scheme where one transfers their property into a UK limited company so that, on death, the Spanish property is inherited in the UK – in a roundabout way – via the inheritance of the shares of the company.***
>
> *The Spanish Tax Office closed the Equity Release tax avoidance scheme last year, when it was tagged as tax fraud. And more recently, it has also dealt with the scheme that offers UK companies the chance to circumvent Spanish IHT (Tax Binding Consultation **07383-13**) pretty much in the same way, by declaring the following:*
>
> **"…In relation to the tax scheme consisting in legally transferring a property to a UK company, with the sole purpose of avoiding IHT in Spain through relocation of the taxation of the shares of the said company to the UK, there cannot be a favourable response by this Tax Department in relation to the lawfulness of the scheme. Only via the appropriate inspection procedures will the Tax Office be able to establish whether it conforms to the law or, as the case may be, infringe it in which case, the Tax Office will be able to regularize the anomaly by initiating the required procedures to combat tax fraud."**
>
> *These conclusions are hardly consistent with a public promotion of a service whereby if you place your property in a UK company, IHT can be legally avoided.*
>
> ***In fact, the official opinion on the matter embeds a message of warning: if you use this scheme to mitigate IHT, you will have clearly crossed the line separating lawful tax avoidance and illegal tax evasion."***

### Legal Significance
- ✅ Spain's official *Consulta Vinculante* 07383-13 is **the definitive government ruling** against the scheme
- ✅ The author (a licensed Spanish solicitor) publicly called the scheme **"bogus"**
- ✅ The article equates continued use of the scheme with **illegal tax evasion** (not mere avoidance)
- ✅ Published in 2014 — Wincham **continued selling the scheme to new clients after this date**, including Philip Harrison

## CRITICAL LEGAL OBSERVATIONS

### 1. Continuing Duty of Care Breach
Wincham continued selling the scheme **at least a year after** the Hacienda ruling was widely published (2014) and continued for many years thereafter. This demonstrates a wilful or reckless disregard for their clients' positions.

### 2. Potential FCA Breach (FSMA 2000 s.19)
Mark Roach publicly claimed FCA authorisation under reg. 615817 in 2014. A public commenter independently verified this number does not exist on the FCA Register. If Wincham was offering investment/financial services without FCA authorisation, this is a **criminal offence** under s.19 of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000, carrying up to 2 years imprisonment and/or an unlimited fine. **This should be urgently verified against the current FCA register at https://register.fca.org.uk/**

### 3. No Independent Legal Endorsement
The public comment thread at The Olive Press confirmed that **no qualified UK or Spanish lawyer would professionally endorse the scheme**. This suggests Wincham was operating without the independent legal backing that a responsible financial services firm would be required to obtain.

### 4. Misrepresentation (Misrepresentation Act 1967)
If Wincham represented the scheme as legally compliant and IHT-effective after the 2014 Hacienda ruling, this may constitute actionable **fraudulent or negligent misrepresentation** under the Misrepresentation Act 1967, giving rise to rescission rights or damages.
