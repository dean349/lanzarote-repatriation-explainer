BACKGROUND

A. Dean Harrison has assembled a proprietary database, forensic evidence pack, and commercial strategy relating to professional negligence claims against Wincham Accountants Ltd (dissolved) and its successor entities (collectively, "the Wincham Scheme"). Ellis Harrison acts as the Consultant's UK associate and introducing consultant.

B. The Consultant proposes to disclose certain Confidential Information to the Firm for the sole purpose of evaluating a commercial partnership relating to the Wincham Scheme (the "Permitted Purpose").

C. The Parties wish to set out the terms on which such Confidential Information will be disclosed, used, and protected.

1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Confidential Information" means all information disclosed by the Consultant to the Firm (whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means) that relates to:

  • (a) The identity, contact details, company numbers, or addresses of any person included in the Wincham victim database;
  • (b) The forensic methodology used to identify or compile the victim database;
  • (c) The contents of any document in the evidence pack (E-01 to E-13 and any additions);
  • (d) The commercial terms proposed or discussed between the Parties;
  • (e) Any other information marked "Confidential" or "Strictly Private";
  • (f) Any information that a reasonable person in the Firm's position would understand to be confidential.

1.2 "Identified Person" means any director, shareholder, or associated individual whose details appear in any document disclosed by the Consultant.

1.3 "Non-Circumvention Period" means a period of five (5) years from the Effective Date.

1.4 "Personal Data" has the meaning given in the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) as retained by section 3 of the Data Protection Act 2018.

2. NON-DISCLOSURE OBLIGATIONS

2.1 The Firm undertakes that it will:

  • (a) Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential;
  • (b) Not disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Consultant;
  • (c) Use the Confidential Information solely for the Permitted Purpose;
  • (d) Restrict internal access to Confidential Information to those partners, associates, and staff who need to know it for the Permitted Purpose, and shall procure that each such person is bound by confidentiality obligations no less onerous than those set out in this Agreement.

2.2 The obligations in clause 2.1 do not apply to information that:

  • (a) Is or becomes publicly known through no act or omission of the Firm;
  • (b) Was in the Firm's possession prior to disclosure, free of any obligation of confidence;
  • (c) Is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or regulatory authority — provided that the Firm gives the Consultant maximum practicable advance notice before making such disclosure.

3. NON-CIRCUMVENTION

3.1 The Firm agrees that during the Non-Circumvention Period it will not, without the prior written consent of the Consultant:

  • (a) Contact, solicit, approach, or communicate with any Identified Person other than in pursuit of a claim arranged through, and with the knowledge of, the Consultant;
  • (b) Instruct any third party to contact, solicit, or communicate with any Identified Person on the Firm's behalf;
  • (c) Independently replicate, reconstruct, or reverse-engineer the Consultant's data methodology for the purpose of identifying Wincham victims, and then contact those victims without the Consultant's involvement;
  • (d) Refer any Identified Person to another law firm or claims management company without the Consultant's prior written consent.

3.2 For the avoidance of doubt, clause 3.1 applies even if:

  • (a) The Firm independently discovers an Identified Person through other means after the date of this Agreement;
  • (b) The Firm claims not to have used the Consultant's Confidential Information in making such contact;
  • (c) The Firm uses an intermediary to make such contact.

3.3 Liquidated Damages. The Parties agree that it would be difficult to quantify the precise loss suffered by the Consultant if the Firm were to breach clause 3.1. The Parties have agreed the following sum as a genuine pre-estimate of loss, having regard to the anticipated Cost Per Acquired Claimant value of each Identified Person. Accordingly, and consistently with the test applied in Cavendish Square Holding BV v Talal El Makdessi; ParkingEye Ltd v Beavis [2015] UKSC 67, the Firm agrees that in the event of any breach of clause 3.1, it shall pay to the Consultant the sum of £2,500 per Identified Person contacted in breach of this Agreement, as liquidated damages. This sum is not a penalty and is not out of all proportion to the Consultant's legitimate commercial interest in the database. This right is in addition to and not in substitution for any other remedy available to the Consultant.

4. NON-SOLICITATION

4.1 During the Non-Circumvention Period, the Firm shall not, without the prior written consent of the Consultant:

  • (a) Solicit, induce, or attempt to induce the Consultant, or any person introduced to the Firm by the Consultant, to terminate or reduce their engagement with the Consultant;
  • (b) Engage, employ, or contract with the Consultant's collaborators, data suppliers, or sub-contractors who have been identified to the Firm in the course of discussions under this Agreement.

5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND DATABASE RIGHTS

5.1 Nothing in this Agreement transfers any intellectual property rights in the Confidential Information from the Consultant to the Firm.

5.2 The Consultant asserts and the Firm acknowledges that the victim database constitutes a protected database within the meaning of the Copyright and Rights in Databases Regulations 1997 (SI 1997/3032) by virtue of the substantial investment made in obtaining, verifying, and presenting its contents. Unauthorised extraction or re-utilisation of a substantial part of the database constitutes an infringement of the Consultant's database right.

5.3 The Firm shall not extract or re-utilise any substantial part of the database for any purpose other than the Permitted Purpose without a separate written licence from the Consultant.

6. DATA PROTECTION

6.1 The Parties acknowledge that the Confidential Information disclosed under this Agreement includes Personal Data within the meaning of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

6.2 The Consultant warrants that the victim database was assembled from publicly available sources, including the Companies House register (maintained under the Companies Act 2006), and that processing for the purpose of pursuing professional negligence claims on behalf of affected individuals constitutes processing in pursuit of a legitimate interest within the meaning of Article 6(1)(f) UK GDPR, having conducted a Legitimate Interests Assessment in accordance with the guidance of the Information Commissioner's Office.

6.3 The Firm undertakes that it shall:

  • (a) Process any Personal Data disclosed to it only for the Permitted Purpose and for the purpose of providing legal services to identified claimants;
  • (b) Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect such Personal Data against accidental loss, destruction, damage, alteration, or unauthorised disclosure;
  • (c) Not transfer any Personal Data to a third country or international organisation without the prior written consent of the Consultant and without ensuring adequate safeguards are in place in accordance with Chapter V UK GDPR;
  • (d) Comply in all respects with its obligations as a Data Controller under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018;
  • (e) Notify the Consultant without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours if it becomes aware of a Personal Data breach affecting any Confidential Information disclosed under this Agreement.

6.4 As between the Parties, the Consultant shall act as Data Controller in respect of the victim database. The Firm shall act as a separate, independent Data Controller in respect of any Personal Data it processes for the purpose of providing legal services to claimants.

7.1 Claims Management Activity. The Parties acknowledge that certain activities described in this Agreement may constitute "regulated claims management activity" within the meaning of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA) as amended by the Financial Guidance and Claims Act 2018, and as defined in the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Claims Management Activity) Order 2018 (SI 2018/1253). The Firm is solely responsible for satisfying itself that its participation in any commercial arrangement arising from this Agreement complies with all applicable rules of the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA), including where applicable the ICOBS and CMCOB sourcebooks. The Firm confirms that it holds or will obtain such FCA authorisation or SRA exemption as may be required before conducting any regulated activity in connection with this Agreement.

7.2 SRA Code of Conduct 2019. The Firm confirms that it is regulated by the Solicitors Regulation Authority (SRA) and that it shall conduct any commercial arrangement with the Consultant in full compliance with the SRA Code of Conduct 2019, including paragraph 5 (referral and fee arrangements) and any applicable transparency rules. The Firm shall make all required disclosures to its clients regarding any referral arrangement.

7.3 Anti-Money Laundering. The Firm acknowledges its obligations under the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 (POCA) and the Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017 (SI 2017/692) (as amended). The Firm shall conduct such client due diligence (CDD) and, where required, enhanced due diligence (EDD), as is required by the 2017 Regulations before accepting any instructions from claimants identified through this Agreement.

7.4 Nothing in this Agreement shall require or be construed as requiring either Party to act in a manner inconsistent with applicable legal, regulatory, or professional obligations.

8. RETURN OF INFORMATION

8.1 On written demand by the Consultant at any time, or automatically on termination of commercial discussions between the Parties, the Firm shall promptly:

  • (a) Return all Confidential Information in tangible form to the Consultant;
  • (b) Permanently delete all electronic copies of Confidential Information from all systems and confirm that no copies have been retained;
  • (c) Certify in writing to the Consultant that it has done so within five (5) business days.

9. TERM

9.1 This Agreement shall come into force on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until:

  • (a) The Parties enter into a superseding commercial agreement incorporating confidentiality terms no less onerous than these; or
  • (b) Either Party provides 30 days' written notice of termination — provided that all non-disclosure, non-circumvention, and data protection obligations shall survive termination for the full Non-Circumvention Period of five (5) years, and data protection obligations shall survive indefinitely to the extent required by law.

10. REMEDIES

10.1 The Firm acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may cause the Consultant irreparable harm for which damages would be an inadequate remedy.

10.2 The Consultant shall, in addition to any other remedy, be entitled to seek interim injunctive relief or other equitable relief from the courts of England and Wales under the established jurisdiction of the court, without the need to prove actual loss.

11.2 The Parties irrevocably submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales to settle any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement.
