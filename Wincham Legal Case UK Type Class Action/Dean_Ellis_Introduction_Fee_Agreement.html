BACKGROUND

A. Dean is the owner of a proprietary database relating to potential professional negligence claimants connected to the Wincham Accountants scheme ("the Wincham Victim Database").

B. Dean intends to enter into a Data Licence Agreement with one or more UK law firms or claims management companies for commercial consideration ("the Data Licence").

C. Ellis has agreed to act as Dean's commercial agent and introducer in the United Kingdom, to identify suitable Licensee firms and to facilitate the execution of the Data Licence.

D. This Agreement records the terms on which Ellis will be remunerated by Dean for that introduction and facilitation.

1. APPOINTMENT

1.1 Dean appoints Ellis as his non-exclusive commercial agent and introducer in the United Kingdom for the purpose of identifying, approaching, and facilitating negotiations with potential Licensees under the Data Licence.

1.2 Ellis accepts this appointment and agrees to act in good faith and in Dean's best commercial interests in conducting introductions.

1.3 Ellis shall not hold himself out as having authority to bind Dean to any agreement, and shall not execute any contract on Dean's behalf without Dean's prior written approval.

2. ELLIS'S ROLE

2.1 Ellis agrees to: - (a) Identify and approach suitable UK law firms or claims management companies as potential Licensees; - (b) Present Dean's pitch materials under the terms of the Non-Disclosure Agreement previously executed; - (c) Negotiate indicative commercial terms on Dean's behalf, subject to Dean's final written approval; - (d) Facilitate execution of the NDA and Data Licence Agreement between Dean and the Licensee; - (e) Act as Dean's point of contact in the United Kingdom for communications with the Licensee.

2.2 Ellis shall not: - (a) Contact individuals within the Wincham Victim Database directly for the purpose of referring them to any law firm; - (b) Represent himself as a data owner or as having any independent rights over the database; - (c) Enter into any fee arrangement with a Licensee on his own account without Dean's prior written consent; - (d) Make any warranty or representation on Dean's behalf beyond those set out in the Data Licence Agreement.

3. INTRODUCTION FEE

3.1 Calculation. In consideration of Ellis's services under this Agreement, Dean agrees to pay Ellis an Introduction Fee of 30% of the total gross consideration actually received by Dean from the Licensee under the Data Licence Agreement, including:

  • (a) The Upfront Licence Fee (clause 5.1(a) of the Data Licence Agreement);
  • (b) The Exclusivity Premium, if any (clause 5.1(b) of the Data Licence Agreement);
  • (c) Each Deferred Milestone Payment as and when received (clause 5.2 of the Data Licence Agreement).

3.2 Example (based on recommended figures — for illustration only):

Dean Receives Ellis (30%) Dean (70%)
Upfront Licence Fee: £25,000 £7,500 £17,500
Exclusivity Premium: £18,750 £5,625 £13,125
Milestone 1 (20 LoCs issued): £31,250 £9,375 £21,875
Milestone 2 (GLO / 50 retainers): £50,000 £15,000 £35,000
Total: £125,000 £37,500 £87,500

3.3 Payment Timing. Dean shall pay Ellis his Introduction Fee within 21 days of receiving cleared funds from the Licensee for each payment under the Data Licence Agreement.

3.4 No deduction. Dean shall not deduct any costs, expenses, or taxes from the calculation of Ellis's fee without Ellis's prior written consent.

3.5 Single Firm. This Agreement applies to the first Licensee introduced by Ellis who executes a Data Licence Agreement. If Ellis introduces further Licensees and Dean enters into additional Data Licence Agreements, the Parties shall agree fees in writing for each additional transaction.

4. EXPENSES

4.1 Ellis shall be responsible for his own out-of-pocket expenses incurred in performing this Agreement (travel, postage, printing etc.) unless Dean has specifically agreed in advance in writing to reimburse a particular expense.

5. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR

5.1 Ellis is an independent contractor and not an employee, partner, or agent of Dean for any purpose other than as expressly stated in this Agreement.

5.2 Ellis is solely responsible for his own tax affairs, National Insurance contributions, and any other statutory obligations arising from sums received under this Agreement.

5.3 Nothing in this Agreement creates any partnership, joint venture, or fiduciary relationship between the Parties.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 Each Party agrees to keep the terms of this Agreement and all related commercial discussions strictly confidential.

6.2 Ellis agrees to keep confidential all information relating to the Wincham Victim Database, the Data Licence Agreement, and Dean's commercial strategy, in accordance with the obligations previously agreed under the Non-Disclosure and Non-Circumvention Agreement.

7. TERM AND TERMINATION

7.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue until the earlier of: - (a) All Introduction Fees have been paid following receipt of all payments under the Data Licence Agreement; or - (b) Termination by either Party on 14 days' written notice.

7.2 Termination shall not affect Ellis's right to receive Introduction Fees in respect of payments received by Dean after the date of termination, if those payments relate to Data Licence Agreements introduced by Ellis before termination.

8. GENERAL

8.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding Ellis's introduction fee and supersedes all prior discussions.

8.2 Variation. No variation shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties.

8.3 Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by the law of England and Wales. The Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

8.4 Third Party Rights. Nothing in this Agreement confers rights on any third party under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999.
