I, Philip Harrison, am writing this email to myself as a personal record of a serious financial mistake made by my accountants, Wincham Accountants (and their successor firm, Adrem Accounting).

I am writing this while events are fresh so that there is a clear, honest record of what happened and when.

BACKGROUND

I am the sole director and a 50% shareholder of Los Romeros Limited (Company Number 06993349), a UK limited company. The company owned a property in Lanzarote, Spain.

Wincham Accountants acted as the company secretary and accountants for Los Romeros Limited. They were responsible for managing all aspects of the company, including arranging the sale of the Lanzarote property.

THE SALE

The Lanzarote property was sold at a Spanish notary on approximately 20 March 2026. The sale price was 315,000 euros. A mandatory 3% tax was withheld by the Spanish tax authority (approximately 9,450 euros), leaving net proceeds of approximately 305,550 euros. This money belonged to Los Romeros Limited as a company. It was not my personal money.

THE ERROR

Before the sale was completed, I was directed by Wincham / Adrem to give a personal UK bank account number to the Spanish notary. I gave them my personal HSBC bank account details, as instructed.

As a result, on or around 27 March 2026, the net sale proceeds (approximately 305,550 euros, converted to approximately 258,000 to 265,000 pounds) were paid directly into my personal HSBC bank account.

Nobody told me that:

1. The money legally belonged to Los Romeros Limited, not to me personally
2. Receiving company money into my personal HSBC account creates a tax problem with HMRC (called a Section 455 charge) which could result in a large additional tax bill
3. I should have had a business bank account in the company's name to receive this money
4. The failure to do this properly would make it significantly more difficult and expensive to close down the company through the proper legal process (called a Members Voluntary Liquidation, or MVL)

WHAT SHOULD HAVE HAPPENED

Wincham and Adrem, as the company's professional advisers, were responsible for making sure the sale proceeds went into a company bank account in the name of Los Romeros Limited. This never happened. No business bank account was ever opened. No loan agreement was ever put in place. No tax advice was ever given to me about what would happen if company money ended up in my personal HSBC account.

THE IMPACT

As a result of this error, I now face:

1. A potential additional HMRC tax charge on the 258,000 to 265,000 pounds sitting in my personal HSBC account
2. A much more complicated and expensive process to close down Los Romeros Limited
3. The risk that HMRC treats the money as personal income taxed at a high rate, rather than as a capital gain taxed at the lower rate

STATEMENT

I confirm that everything I have written above is true and accurate to the best of my knowledge and recollection. I intend to take independent legal and tax advice about this matter.

Philip Harrison
Director, Los Romeros Limited (Company Number 06993349)
Sheffield, United Kingdom
