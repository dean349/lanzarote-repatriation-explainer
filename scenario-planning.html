Scenario A — MVL Dissolution (Recommended)

✓ Strongly Recommended
Senior Adviser Verdict

Wind up the company via MVL. Do not buy more property inside it.

The Members' Voluntary Liquidation (MVL) legally converts the company cash into a capital distribution, taxed at CGT rates (18–24%) rather than Income Tax rates (up to 39.35%). For a retired basic-rate taxpayer, this is the single most efficient extraction method available in UK law.

The Spanish CGT is already fixed — nothing can reduce it now. The only question left is how to get the remaining ∼£257,000 out of Los Romeros Limited as cheaply as possible. The MVL achieves that.

The Spanish Reinvestment Relief Myth — Explained Clearly

Spain has a vivienda habitual reinvestment exemption that allows sellers to defer CGT if they reinvest in a new primary residence. However, this relief is only available to:

  • Sellers who are Spanish tax residents
  • Properties that are the seller’s primary home

Philip is a UK resident selling via a corporate wrapper. Neither condition is met. This relief is entirely unavailable to him. There is no legal mechanism to roll the Spanish CGT gain into a new property purchase to defer or eliminate it.

⚠ Long-Run Warning Buying another property is a deferral strategy that increases long-run total tax, complexity, and risk. The only scenario where it makes sense is if Philip genuinely wants to continue holding property for lifestyle or rental income reasons — and even then, holding property personally after the MVL is far simpler and cheaper.

Three Levers That Still Improve the MVL Outcome

Even in the recommended MVL scenario, there are meaningful steps Philip can take now to increase his net proceeds.

1. Maximise the Director’s Loan Before MVL

The Director’s Loan Account (DLA) stands at £25,069. Every additional pound added to this account is recovered tax-free at MVL (it is repayment of a debt, not a distribution). Philip should document every personal payment he has made for Los Romeros Limited since 31 August 2025:

  • Insurance premiums
  • Utility bills or service charges
  • Estate agent or management fees
  • Any professional fees paid personally on the company’s behalf
💰 Tax Saving Every £1,000 added to the DLA saves £180–£240 in personal CGT that would otherwise be due on the MVL distribution. It is the highest-value paperwork exercise available.

2. Split the MVL Across Two Tax Years

The Insolvency Practitioner has flexibility on when distributions are made. If the MVL straddles 5 April (end of tax year), Philip can make a partial distribution in the current tax year (using £3,000 CGT allowance) and the remainder in the next year (using another £3,000 allowance).

ⓘ Small But Easy Win Doubling the CGT annual exemption saves approximately £540–£720. Costs nothing to implement — just needs to be planned with the IP before the first distribution.

3. Use a Specialist FX Broker (Not a High-Street Bank)

The company holds euros. Converting at a retail bank costs 2–4% in exchange spread. A specialist FX broker charges 0.3–0.6%.
