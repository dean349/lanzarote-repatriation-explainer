How Spanish Capital Gains Tax Works

When a non-resident sells a property in Spain, the Spanish government charges IRNR (Impuesto sobre la Renta de No Residentes) — Spain’s equivalent of Capital Gains Tax for foreign property owners.

Step 1 — At the Notary on Completion Day (Modelo 211)

The buyer’s solicitor automatically withholds 3% of the sale price and pays it to AEAT within 30 days. This is a deposit — not the final tax.

ⓘ Key FactOn a €320,000 sale: €9,600 is withheld automatically on the day of completion.

Step 2 — Within 3 Months of Sale (Modelo 210)

Philip files a Modelo 210 return with a Spanish asesor fiscal, showing all deductions and the final tax at 19%.

OutcomeWhat Happens
Final tax < €9,600Spain refunds the difference (6–18 months)
Final tax > €9,600Philip pays the balance within 3 months
